Will Barry Keoghan return as the joker in Batman 2?

May 2024 Â· 3 minute read

The anticipation for the sequel to "The Batman" has been steadily building, and one of the burning questions on the minds of fans is whether Barry Keoghan will reprise his role as the Joker in "The Batman 2".

Keoghan's cryptic responses and the hints dropped in various interviews have left fans speculating about the return of this iconic character. 

Barry Keoghan's Involvement

Barry Keoghan, the talented Irish actor, made a brief but memorable cameo as the Joker in "The Batman".

Despite not featuring in the final cut, his portrayal of the iconic Gotham villain in a deleted scene left a lasting impression on audiences.

In a recent interview with Vanity Fair, Keoghan seemingly confirmed his return as the Joker in the sequel by not denying his involvement, essentially hinting at his reprisal of the role.

Director's Perspective

Director Matt Reeves' approach to the character of the Joker in "The Batman" universe is crucial in understanding the potential for Keoghan's return.

While the ending of "The Batman" hinted at a possible rivalry between Batman and Joker, Reeves has expressed interest in exploring more of this version of the Joker's story. But Reeves has also been non-committal about Keoghan's return, leaving the door open for speculation. This ambiguity adds to the intrigue surrounding the character's future in the franchise.

Script Development and Storyline

The script for "The Batman 2" is still in development, and the question arises whether Joker will end up as the main antagonist in the sequel.

The ending of "The Batman" set up an organic way for Joker and Bruce Wayne to go up against each other, and even the deleted scene provided another opportunity to establish their rivalry in the sequel.

But considering Reeves' interest in exploring lesser-known villains and the potential similarity with previous Batman movies, the direction of the storyline regarding the Joker remains uncertain.

Fan Anticipation

The possibility of Barry Keoghan's return as the Joker has left fans eagerly awaiting more news about "The Batman 2".

Keoghan's cryptic responses and the hints dropped in interviews have generated significant excitement and speculation within the fan community.

The prospect of witnessing the Joker come to life again on the big screen has only heightened the anticipation for the sequel.

Potential Risks and Alternatives

While the return of the Joker would undoubtedly excite fans, there are potential risks associated with featuring the character as the main antagonist in "The Batman 2".

Reeves' success with "The Batman" was attributed to his choice of allowing a different villain, The Riddler, to take the spotlight, offering a fresh take on Batman's rogues' gallery.

So, it's plausible that Reeves may opt to focus on other lesser-known villains who have not been featured in live-action movies before, rather than bringing the Joker to the forefront.

Release Date and Production

"The Batman 2" is scheduled for release on October 2, 2026, with director Matt Reeves and the cast, including Robert Pattinson, gearing up for the highly anticipated sequel. While production delays have affected the initial filming schedule, the sequel's development continues to generate buzz and speculation about the characters and storyline.

 

Overall, the potential return of Barry Keoghan as the Joker in "The Batman 2" remains a topic of great interest and speculation among fans.

The hints dropped by Keoghan and the director, combined with the ongoing development of the script, have fueled excitement and anticipation for the sequel.

Whether the Joker will assume a central role in the storyline or take a backseat to other lesser-known villains is a question that continues to captivate the imaginations of fans eagerly awaiting the next installment in the "The Batman" universe.

The uncertain but intriguing prospect of Barry Keoghan's return as the Joker in "The Batman 2" has sparked fervent discussions and heightened anticipation for the sequel's release.
