The Rise, Fall &amp; Reboot Of Witches Of WeHo Wine Explained

September 2024 · 3 minute read

With the popularity of Scandoval reigniting interest in Vanderpump Rules, the Witches of WeHo may decide to bring back their controversial wine line.

The Witches of WeHo (Kristen Doute, Katie Maloney, and Stassi Schroeder) have decided to relaunch their Vanderpump Rules-themed wine. Thanks to its ups and downs, there has always been a saga element to this wine company. Even though the three former best friends have gone their separate ways, two of the witches have brought back the brand amid "Scandoval."

In 2019, Vanderpump Rules fans learned that Stassi, Katie, and Kristen would be launching a wine line, It would be based on the self-proclaimed nickname they'd given themselves, back when all of them lived in West Hollywood. The three reality TV stars first launched the Witches of WeHo's Basic Witch Potion #1 Pinot Grigio, as well as a rosé. However, in June 2020, the wine line was pulled. This happened around the same time that Stassi and Kristen were fired over a scandal. The controversy involved false accusations about a co-star, Faith Stowers.

When Did Witches Of WeHo Wine Launch?

During the early days of Vanderpump Rules, Stassi, Kristen, and Katie were not only servers at SUR - they were best friends. The 20-something gal pals were the main (and sometimes mean) "girl group" at Lisa Vanderpump's restaurant. They liked to think they called the shots. The three stars enjoyed pushing around a young Scheana Shay, and gave their clique a unique name. That's how The Witches of WeHo group was born. They rode bikes around their neighborhood, instead of broomsticks.

Why The Vanderpump Rules Wine Brand Was So Controversial

As stated above, after it was revealed that Stassi and Kristen had made insensitive racial remarks about Faith, the wine line was pulled from shelves. At the time, a representative from Nocking Point (the company that manufactured the wine) said it would no longer be producing the Vanderpump Rules elixir. The wine manufacturer also released a statement via Us Weekly, part of which said that the firm stood, "squarely against the systemic oppression."

Are The "Witches" Cashing In On The Scandoval?

A little more than a month after the news broke regarding Tom Sandoval cheating on Ariana Madix, The Witches of WeHo wine made its return to stores. This time, Stassi has bowed out of the venture, with Kristen and Katie taking the reins. Vanderpump Rules fans can buy two bottles for $44 at the Nocking Point website. The secret ingredient for this witch's brew happens to be, "The tears of your exes!"

Sources: Us Weekly, Nocking Point
