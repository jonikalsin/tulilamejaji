Where are the 2024 and 2028 Olympics?

May 2024 · 4 minute read

The awarding of the Olympics is a prestigious global event that garners immense attention and anticipation. The process of selecting host cities for the Olympics involves a rigorous evaluation of the candidates' capabilities and resources.

In 2017, the International Olympic Committee (IOC) made a groundbreaking decision regarding the allocation of the 2024 and 2028 Summer Games. This decision has significant implications for the cities of Paris and Los Angeles, which are set to host these historic events.

Also, the upcoming 2022 Winter Games in Beijing and the 2026 Winter Games in Milan and Cortina d’Ampezzo also play a crucial part in the Olympic landscape. 

The 2024 Olympics: Paris

Paris, known for its rich history and cultural significance, has been awarded the honor of hosting the 2024 Summer Olympics.

This decision comes after Paris faced close losses in previous bids, making the upcoming event particularly significant. The city's proposal emphasizes sustainability and environmental responsibility, aligning the event with the Paris Agreement on Climate Change.

This commitment includes reducing the Games’ carbon emissions by 50%, offsetting residual emissions, and advocating for long-term carbon compensation projects.

Paris's Approach to Hosting the Olympics

One notable aspect of Paris's bid is its utilization of existing or temporary structures for 95% of the sporting venues. This approach not only emphasizes sustainability but also addresses the post-Olympic challenges that host cities often face.

The city's innovative plan includes staging events at iconic locations such as Roland Garros for tennis and boxing, the Champ-de-Mars for beach volleyball, and Versailles for equestrian events.

The 2028 Olympics: Los Angeles

Los Angeles, a city with a rich Olympic history, is set to host the 2028 Summer Olympics. This will mark the third time that Los Angeles has taken on the responsibility of organizing the Games, with previous successful hostings in 1932 and 1984.

What sets Los Angeles's approach apart is its intention to rely on existing sports parks in the area, minimizing the need for new construction. The University of California, Los Angeles is designated to serve as the Athletes' Village, showcasing the city's commitment to efficient and sustainable planning.

Considerations for Los Angeles

In considering the 2028 Olympics, Los Angeles has embraced a strategy that prioritizes the use of already established infrastructure. This approach aligns with the city's vision for a cost-effective and environmentally conscious event.

By building no new structures, Los Angeles aims to demonstrate the feasibility of hosting the Olympics without significant financial burden or environmental impact.

The IOC's Decision and Implications

The decision by the International Olympic Committee to simultaneously award both the 2024 and 2028 Summer Games is unprecedented.

This approach was taken to accommodate the strong candidacies of both Paris and Los Angeles, effectively avoiding a situation where one city would lose out entirely. The decision reflects a recognition of the capabilities and eagerness of these two cities to host the Games, thus ensuring the continuity and stability of the Olympic movement.

Impacts of the Decision

The simultaneous awarding of the 2024 and 2028 Olympics has far-reaching implications. It demonstrates the IOC's commitment to flexibility and adaptability, particularly in response to the evolving global landscape.

By granting both cities the opportunity to host the Games, the IOC aims to foster a spirit of collaboration and inclusivity, setting a new precedent in the history of the Olympic Games.

Beyond 2024 and 2028: Broader Context of the Olympics

The Olympics are a global phenomenon that transcends individual host cities and years. Looking beyond the upcoming Games, it is essential to recognize the broader context of the Olympic movement. The 2022 Winter Games in Beijing and the 2026 Winter Games in Milan and Cortina d’Ampezzo are also significant milestones in the Olympic calendar.

Beijing 2022 Winter Games

The 2022 Winter Games in Beijing have garnered attention due to controversies surrounding human rights violations and calls for boycotts by governments and activist groups. Despite these challenges, the IOC has maintained its stance of remaining politically neutral, emphasizing the continuation of the Games as planned.

Milan-Cortina 2026 Winter Games

The 2026 Winter Games, to be hosted by Milan and Cortina d’Ampezzo, represent a return to Italy for the global competition. Italy's commitment to sustainability is evident in its plan to utilize existing or temporary structures for 13 out of 14 venues, aligning with the IOC's vision for environmentally responsible and cost-effective Olympics.

 

The allocation of the 2024 and 2028 Olympics to Paris and Los Angeles signifies a new chapter in the history of the Games. These dynamic host cities are poised to demonstrate innovative and sustainable approaches to organizing the world's most celebrated sporting event.

As the Olympic movement continues to evolve, it is essential to consider the broader implications and the ongoing global conversation surrounding the Games. The upcoming Olympics serve as a testament to the enduring spirit of unity, athleticism, and international cooperation that define the Olympic Games.
